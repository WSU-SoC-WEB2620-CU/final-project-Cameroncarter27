Crossy Road

Crossy Road is an infinite runner game in the same vein as Subway Surfers or Temple Run and takes significant influence from the classic arcade game Frogger. The game is a play on the classic chicken crossing the road joke and as such it focuses on the player trying to make their way across busy streets, train tracks, and rivers. Crossy Road started as a phone game but was eventually altered and turned into an arcade game. The arcade version of this game is one that I have a significant amount of experience with. As such, I have found it to be one of the best, if not the best, games for racking up high amounts of tickets.

To get the full value out of each game the player must beat a certain score which then rewards them with a bonus that stacks on top of the player’s current score. I have seen this bonus range from 250 to 1,000 extra tickets on top of the score of around 150 to 200 that it takes for the player to achieve this bonus. It is because of this high volume of tickets that is achievable each game that this particular game has become by far my favorite. From what I have seen it is the most consistent if you are looking to save up tickets over time. In my time playing this game there have been some fundamental tips that I employ every game to help me achieve my high scores. Those tips are the following.

  • Stay in the middle

    Always try to stay in the middle of the screen. This helps to prevent you from being caught off guard by a car coming on screen or log moving too fast off of the screen.

  • Be patient but decisive

    When approaching a road with several lanes you don’t want to rush into the road unless there is immediately a clear path. Take a little bit of time to plan a route or wait for a gap to open. When you do decide to go, try not to hesitate when you see a path but also don’t be afraid to change your route on the fly if things play out differently than you intended.

  • Be careful with train tracks

    One of the more common ways for a run to end is by not being careful with the trains. While most other aspects of this game can be overcome through skill, with trains that isn’t always the case as even if you’re careful they can still screw up a game. So, to minimize the chances of getting hit by a train you should first see if there’s a stretch of land before the tracks that you can stand on away from danger. If possible you will want to get at least one train to activate as the track that that train was on will now be train free and safe for awhile.

    If you can’t wait for a train to go when there are multiple tracks then always start by standing on the first track as this strategy gives you the most options for escape. For example, if the track you’re on lights up then you just need to hop to the next one while if the track in front of you lights up then you’re already good to go! There are scenarios where luck may not be on your side and all tracks light up at once. In this scenario the only thing you can do is hurry and get off the tracks but you have to be quick. In general though the strategies I’ve outlined here are the best ways to go.

Quik Drop

Quik Drop is a game that revolves around dropping all 50 allotted balls into several moving holes. The more balls that you make into the holes, the more tickets you get. You want to make sure to go fast as each round is timed so you can’t take too long or the game will end. Another key aspect of this game is timing. If you drop a ball in the wrong spot then it’ll miss or bounce away from the cylinder thus ruining any chance of getting the jackpot. To get the jackpot on this game you have to get every single one of the 50 balls into the holes. This task can prove to be pretty difficult because, depending on the the current jackpot value, the time limit provided to drop the balls could be as low as 20-22 seconds with it eventually maxing at 30 seconds.

The time limit will eventually start to increase as more people play the game without winning the jackpot. If the jackpot isn’t won during a certain game then it’s value will increase for the next game until someone eventually wins it. If the value of the jackpot is in the 500 to around 650/700 range then in most locations the game will be in a sort of “hard mode” as the time limit will be at it’s lowest. When the game is in this mode the player will have to drop 4 balls in 10 of the holes and 5 in the other two. The timing on this is tight but it is possible to win every round if you get it down.

As the jackpot gets past the 650/700 range then it becomes easier as you will now only have to get 5 balls in one hole and 4 balls in the other 11. This mode lasts until the jackpot is around 800 and then it gets easier again. In the final mode when the jackpot is anywhere in the 800 to 1000 range you should only have to get 4 balls in the 12 holes without having to worry about sneaking in a 5th.

If you are able to get these patterns down then this game is one of the best for saving up tickets. The games on this machine go faster than Crossy Road games so if you are able to get consistent with this game then it will allow you to build up tickets extremely fast but just potentially at a higher cost than if you were to focus your time on playing something like Crossy Road.

Deal or No Deal

Deal or No Deal is a seemingly luck based game in which you are shown several suitcases with various ticket values. You are then tasked with trying to follow the highest value case around so you can choose it as your case and get the highest ticket value possible. The problem is that the cases are shuffled around at high speeds thus making it almost impossible to follow with the naked eye. This makes the outcome of this game often subpar as you have to be very lucky or have a very keen eye to end up getting the higher value cases.

However, if you are looking to get the highest value case each game there is a little trick that you can employ. While it is kind of a hack it does work pretty good. The trick here is to pull out your phone and record the animation of the cases getting shuffled around. You can then take this video and make the cases significantly easier to follow by putting the video in slow motion. You’ll want to make sure your video is slow enough to allow you to follow the case but fast enough that you can watch it and find the desired cases position before the allotted time to pick your case is up.

If you do miss the time frame to pick your case then it’s not the end of the world. You probably will end up with a lower amount of tickets as the final deal given by the banker will be an average of the two cases left. This basically makes it impossible for it to be higher than the highest value case but it will still give you a respectable amount tickets.

In terms of overall ticket output, the other games mentioned on this page will usually provide you with significantly more tickets if you are able to master them. However, if the more skill based games really aren’t your cup of tea but you still want a good amount of tickets then this is pretty solid game to check out.
